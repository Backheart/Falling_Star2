As the man stood on the hill, he gazed out upon a world in utter chaos. Flames engulfed the landscape, consuming everything in their path. The once blue skies were now a deep, foreboding grey, filled with the smoke and ashes of destruction. The sounds of screams and wailing filled the air, as people ran frantically, seeking escape from the inferno. But it was too late. The damage was done. The world was ending, and it was all because of the wrong decisions that had been made. The rejection of Jesus, the one who had come to save humanity from itself, had sealed their fate.

The man thought back to the times when Jesus had walked among them, teaching of love and compassion, of forgiveness and redemption. But they had rejected him, choosing instead to follow their own selfish desires and pride. Now, as the world burned around them, they realized too late the error of their ways. They had been warned, but they had refused to listen. And now, it was too late. But the man on the hill was not like the others. He had always believed in Jesus, and he had prepared for this moment. He turned and ran towards a spaceship that was waiting for him, its engines roaring as it prepared for takeoff. As he boarded the ship, the man felt a sense of relief wash over him. He had escaped the destruction of the world, and he would live on to tell the tale of what had happened.
